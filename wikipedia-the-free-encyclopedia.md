## From today's featured article

In September 1826, [**Margaret Warden died**](/broken/pages/c5747523656f8ac5fb5ef0e8e5f2dd68a28f7497) by [arsenic poisoning](/broken/pages/0d16fff83dca6651ea26f8ba8885be0dedc62735), near [Dundee](/broken/pages/870c18d0f7dceeb7f6f5de83f9c69fad4beca261), Scotland. Warden, a young servant girl, was pregnant at the time of her death; the father was George Smith, the son of Warden's employers, Mary and David Smith. The attending doctor thought Warden died of [cholera](/broken/pages/a2d01a8cbdb04e26c36ef98164504573e8a25426), but rumours of poisoning soon spread and she was exhumed. Her stomach contents were tested; [arsenic](/broken/pages/b25ae32d0dead20df09c6a0e97b0a42739e163f5) was found, and in October Mary Smith was committed for trial for murder. Her defence advocates called nearly fifty witnesses, and the trial began on 19 February. Testimony at the trial made it clear that Smith had given Warden something to drink shortly before she became ill. The defence called witnesses who testified that Warden might have committed suicide. The jury returned a verdict of [not proven](/broken/pages/58906709bcc4b37db543f681d26be3efebd540eb), acquitting Smith of the crime. Popular opinion at the time was that Smith was guilty, and ballads were written about the case. One of Smith's lawyers later wrote that he was sure she had committed the murder. [Ilia Malinin](/broken/pages/370c97c45581581ff18e44e04ce0c54fc8eaf1af), three-time U.S. national champion in [men's single skating](/broken/pages/2f6c7600abb2bfc2c61b963317cafac4c370fd6d)

The [**U.S. Figure Skating Championships**](/broken/pages/c59d8d0059394f834964d155a2fb89e3bd3e5ecc) are an annual [figure skating competition](/broken/pages/50e34291a7b12dc940a3cb1c642cb04e71c83dba) to crown the national champions of the United States in [figure skating](/broken/pages/2bf7b35f1af7d98741300d1f15336001fa12f48d). Organized by [U.S. Figure Skating](/broken/pages/4210e382c8b46ca90f39babf20a50fa3ae50744c), medals are awarded in [men's singles, women's singles](/broken/pages/2f6c7600abb2bfc2c61b963317cafac4c370fd6d), [pair skating](/broken/pages/d4e87aefb2fdb98c119a08188d6228b7982667f5) and [ice dance](/broken/pages/452eccdc753ca5d2b9d74643926b5853fee83f4d), at the senior and junior levels. The first U.S. Championships were held in 1914 in [New Haven, Connecticut](/broken/pages/79f34ff2dc228e5d796763e3b164176eb9d21f2d); while they were interrupted during World War I, they have been held without interruption since 1920. [Dick Button](/broken/pages/3496864275f718acec0af74f6e4d619f62c127cb) and [Roger Turner](/broken/pages/8e1420abbddd789a729611b9597c569720b77c24) are tied for winning the most U.S. Championships titles in men's singles (with seven each), while [Maribel Vinson](/broken/pages/db411bd73a28e4824af75dc17655c98702ae127d) and [Michelle Kwan](/broken/pages/6357fb920e1e57d04872ea7b90557d3bc3674b11) are tied for winning the most titles in women's singles (with nine each). [Theresa Weld-Blanchard](/broken/pages/c5033ec85a16b75908799e7b488d1e1c4e121aeb) and [Nathaniel Niles](/broken/pages/20c19562ac0896ba42c1c07b8be7c0642b23269f) hold the record in pair skating (with nine), while [Meryl Davis and Charlie White](/broken/pages/1b99267608223ac98815c0ab67158f6def8d48a0), and [Madison Chock](/broken/pages/fd61eeda715d80cb6da899c7fbc5da9d50124239) and [Evan Bates](/broken/pages/fa38afdc56168a97ef594d08ed68c5c5352c652e), are tied for winning the most titles in ice dance (with six each).
