# Wikipedia:About - Wikipedia

"Imagine a world in which every single person on the planet is given free access to the sum of all human knowledge. That's what we're doing." — [Jimmy Wales](/broken/pages/070a3f0fa7e578e9188cc6c1ea93e4412f39bd2c)

[**Wikipedia**](/broken/pages/5117da7420a6937235f127c84000c01261251e32) is a [free online encyclopedia](/broken/pages/33580259aa0306853a7c3525e39f5ab6157c5590) that anyone can edit. It is hosted by the [Wikimedia Foundation](/broken/pages/6b8336c94663d12fb780ddb0f10579f51c2c130f) and written collaboratively by volunteers known as [Wikipedians](/broken/pages/db94ddd454e4beb8e14546c88b986bf74eebf7ec).

Key points

* Purpose: to benefit readers by presenting information on all branches of [knowledge](/broken/pages/93b33175c18f94d1be94521b2d66b42ba1d1c100). ([Wikipedia:Purpose](/broken/pages/f0b1f6dc01a46977db480ea80ffe08263f1ba8be))
* Open editing: anyone with [Internet access](/broken/pages/a5c38313a9b7496f001608d2229b06cd5b3f9b7d) can edit most pages; editing is restricted in limited cases to prevent [disruption](/broken/pages/0b0bb7a64e9374b2432cdb2cb56f5b7836740194) or [vandalism](/broken/pages/d8293968e57fc16cb47145082e4dc7bf0db53ab6). ([Protection policy](/broken/pages/fbf14c306330ac8b0962a6daf7c06537ec5c0434))
* Policies: content must be [verifiable](/broken/pages/4aed141dc1f3029e3f113a091d019dc1e9851f75) by published [reliable sources](/broken/pages/db14ab0912ace516e7bba2a0e3b50de1e2168b3b) and conform to core policies such as neutral point of view and no original research. ([Editing policy](/broken/pages/bb43ee0cbcb507beabf944dae1c0c34f99519127))
* Scale: Wikipedia contains millions of articles across hundreds of languages (see [List of Wikipedias](https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/List_of_Wikipedias#All_Wikipedias_ordered_by_number_of_articles)). English Wikipedia article count and contributor statistics are available through linked resources.

## How Wikipedia works (overview)

* Articles are written collaboratively by volunteers; articles can be edited by most visitors. ([Who writes Wikipedia?](/broken/pages/2abce47ad2bbbfd3a70e1ae242643e3b6e6c1606))
* The quality of content is more important than the contributor’s credentials; experienced editors monitor changes and revert vandalism via [watchlists](/broken/pages/f7dc74a7795e04433bf22d77ba75e43e585278e1) and [recent changes patrol](/broken/pages/1d5a3ad04131494e98b5e3a01e82f9415e709ef8).
* Contributions that are personal opinions, unreviewed research, libellous material, or copyright violations are not allowed and may be removed. Relevant policies are linked above.
* To start editing, click the `Edit` or `Edit source` button (or pencil icon) on a page that is not protected. ([Help:Editing](/broken/pages/d1bb82e006e14b0fa252bdd6a00559d9ec40837a))

## Fundamental principles

Wikipedia’s fundamental principles are summarized in its [five pillars](/broken/pages/b0e5c84d8e50ebdde425fb54ed88a9748a647430). The community has many [policies and guidelines](/broken/pages/967cdaf8c77975c850534c38b4fe2e6631216a95), but new editors do not need to learn them all before contributing.

## Notes

* Wikipedia is continually updated; articles on major news events can appear within minutes.
* The software and community tools make it straightforward to revert errors and to monitor changes.
