## 3 January 2026

### Armed conflicts and attacks

* Operation Southern Spear / War on drugs
  * 2026 United States strikes in Venezuela:
    * U.S. President Donald Trump orders airstrikes on Venezuela, including Caracas, killing at least 40 people (civilians and military). A national state of emergency is declared. (The Times, CNN)

### Law and crime

## 2 January 2026

### Armed conflicts and attacks

* Yemeni civil war (2025 Southern Yemen offensive / Saudi-led intervention)
  * Saudi-led coalition launches airstrikes in Hadhramaut Governorate, killing 20 Southern Transitional Council (STC) fighters and injuring others. (AFP via Le Monde)
  * The STC announces a plan to hold an independence referendum for the State of South Arabia within two years. (Reuters)

### Business and economy

* BYD Company surpasses Tesla as the world's best‑selling electric-vehicle automaker after selling 2.26 million vehicles in 2025 versus Tesla's 1.64 million. (AP, DW)

### Disasters and accidents

* 2026 Guerrero earthquake
  * Mw 6.5 earthquake strikes off the coast of San Marcos, Guerrero, Mexico, killing two and injuring 12. (AFP via The New Zealand Herald, Infobae)
* Bus and truck head-on collision on a federal highway in Pelotas, Rio Grande do Sul, Brazil: 11 killed, 11 injured. (Reuters, G1)
* Multi‑story building collapses in Nairobi, Kenya; at least four trapped. (AP)
* A boat carrying over 200 migrants capsizes off the North Bank Division of the Gambia: dozens missing, at least 102 rescued. (AP)
* A private MD 369FF helicopter crashes in Pinal County, Arizona, U.S.; all four aboard killed. (NBC News, AP)

### Health and environment

* Climate change in the United Kingdom
  * The UK records its hottest and sunniest year in 2025 with an average temperature of 10.09 °C, surpassing the 2022 record. (Business Day)

### Law and crime

* 2022–2025 Pakistan political unrest / Terrorism in Pakistan
  * A Pakistani anti‑terrorism court sentences eight journalists and online commentators, including Wajahat Saeed Khan, Adil Raja, and Shaheen Sehbai, to life imprisonment in absentia for terrorism-related offences tied to digital content supporting Imran Khan. (Reuters)
* 2025–2026 Iranian protests
  * A protester is shot dead by security forces in Harsin, Kermanshah province, Iran. (Hengaw)
* Arrest of Yoon Suk Yeol
  * A South Korean court issues a new six-month detention warrant for former president Yoon Suk Yeol after indicting him on additional charges linked to his 2024 declaration of martial law. (Reuters)

* AI boom / Economy of South Korea

* Migrant boat capsizes in The Gambia: seven killed, 96 rescued, at least 97 missing. (Reuters)

### Law and crime

### Politics and elections

* China imposes a 13% VAT on birth control drugs and devices to address declining birth rates. (Reuters)
* Guy Parmelin is sworn in as President of Switzerland, succeeding Karin Keller‑Sutter. (Swiss Info)

## 30 December 2025

### Armed conflicts and attacks

* Gaza war
  * Israel announces suspension of many international humanitarian organisations from operating in Gaza starting January 1 for allegedly failing to pass new vetting rules and cooperating with Hamas and militant groups. (AP)
* Yemeni civil war (2025 Southern Yemen offensive / Saudi‑led)

* Somali Civil War (2025 Shabelle offensive)
  * Somali Armed Forces kill 29 al‑Shabaab militants in Middle Shabelle. (Reuters)

* Turkey–Islamic State conflict

### Politics and elections

* Guinea: Provisional results show coup leader Mamady Doumbouya elected president. (Reuters)

## 29 December 2025

### Armed conflicts and attacks

* Turkey–Islamic State conflict
  * Three police officers and six IS gunmen killed in a shootout during a raid in Elmalık, Yalova Province, Turkey; eight officers and a civilian wounded. (Al Jazeera)

### Disasters and accidents

* Avalanche at a Pyrenees ski resort in Panticosa, Aragon, Spain: three killed, one injured. (Daily Express)
* Car crash on Lagos–Ibadan Expressway, Ogun State, Nigeria: two killed and boxer Anthony Joshua injured. (BBC News, TVC News)

### International relations

* Cross‑strait relations / Justice Mission 2025
  * China conducts its largest live‑fire exercises to date around Taiwan, designating multiple sea and air zones to rehearse a blockade and signal opposition to recent U.S. arms sales. (Reuters)
* India–New Zealand relations
  * India and New Zealand reach a free trade agreement: India grants zero‑duty access for many exports; New Zealand receives phased concessions on most Indian tariff lines while excluding dairy and selected agricultural products. (AP)

### Law and crime

* Corruption in Malaysia
  * Malaysian Anti‑Corruption Commission raids several companies and freezes six bank accounts investigating alleged bribery linked to army procurement; army chief Muhammad Hafizuddeain Jantan placed on leave pending inquiry. (Reuters)
* War crimes in the Syrian civil war
  * Syrian transitional government orders military to secure a mass grave near Al‑Dumayr and opens a criminal investigation into covert transfers of bodies during the previous administration. (Reuters)
* Mexican drug war
  * Livestreamed mass shooting in Zapopan, Jalisco: three killed and four injured after suspected Jalisco New Generation Cartel gunmen open fire on a Lamborghini. (BNO News)
* Russia
  * Vladimir Putin signs a law allowing the government to disregard criminal judgments by foreign or international courts that lack a treaty basis or UNSC mandate. (Reuters)

### Politics and elections

* 2025 Iranian protests
  * Protests in Tehran over the rial and inflation; Central Bank governor Mohammad Reza Farzi resigns. (AP)
* 2025 Iraqi parliamentary election
  * Haibat al‑Halbousi of the Progress Party is elected Speaker of the Council of Representatives. (Reuters)
