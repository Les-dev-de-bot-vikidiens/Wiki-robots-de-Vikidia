Maule Air

Maule Air, Inc. is a manufacturer of light, single-engined, short take-off and landing (STOL) [aircraft](/broken/pages/e9e05c66b24ed30918c13970a29dff1fe2ca9a41), based in [Moultrie, Georgia](/broken/pages/62abe60f5c4a3f230dc4d0bc75e1c3434c400e6a), U.S. The company delivered 2,500 aircraft in its first 50 years of business.

### History

Belford D. Maule (1911–1995) designed his first aircraft, the [M-1](/broken/pages/c61953b07ca8fab693dd4ac2ad879c5e9212fb21) starting at age 19. He founded the company Mechanical Products Co. in [Napoleon, Michigan](/broken/pages/9db631b70cadb7e431718073068f7663e18fe3b4) to market his own starter design. In 1941 the B.D. Maule Co. was founded,\[1] and Maule produced tailwheels and fabric testers. In 1953 he began design work, and started aircraft production with the "Bee-Dee" M-4 in 1957.\[2]

The company is a family-owned enterprise. Its owner, [June Maule](/broken/pages/026ef253b0f21d533b346d18cc3d9b42de602840), widow of B. D. Maule, remained directly involved with factory production until her death in 2009 at the age of 92.\[3]

### Products

The aircraft produced by Maule Air are [tube-and-fabric](/broken/pages/4628d85203066aecc1c5e9962387d06b5a3b1201) designs and are popular with [bush pilots](/broken/pages/5c7239a767cabcc1cacc8affea48b351393eddcd)\[4] thanks to their very low [stall speed](/broken/pages/755061c9faaa2696470dcf71c6ef76a1d01c1c20), [tundra tires](/broken/pages/74b3bab7dd0076ae68c2f66db3fa79f15714fbcf) and [oleo strut](/broken/pages/daaf2a172bc7f620e2e05e29aa1267ca8d97b534) landing gear. Most Maules are built with [tailwheel](/broken/pages/e4795946eef6476986f21b6af7b88eae9ac60994) or [amphibious](/broken/pages/18ebb5e4486a5651e9600d92e5340022e85d2cd4) configurations, although the newer MXT models have [tricycle gear](/broken/pages/4acf0aa2b485163eaea64fe4e5243a606fa509dd).
